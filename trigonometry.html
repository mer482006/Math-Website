TRIGONOMETRY

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics dealing with relations involving lengths and angles of triangles. It can be used to find the heights of tall building and depth of the oceans, where it is not possible to find the measurement using an equipment. The word ‘trigonometry’ is derived from the Greek words ‘tri’ ( meaning three ), ‘gon’ ( meaning sides ) and ‘metron’ ( meaning measure ).


Pythagoras Theorem and Trigonometic Ratios

Let us consider a right-angled-triangle. By pyth, the side opp. to angle B would be AC which is the hypotenuse. Side BC will be the perpendicular
and AB would be the base. Now, since angle 'A' is denoted by theta (θ), The perpendicular BC would be the opposite side and base AB would be
the adjacent side. The hypotenuse wil remain the same.



QUICK TIP

A mnemonic to remember the ratios: OSHACHOTA

  • Preceding letter of S is "O" and succeding letter is "H", hence
    sin θ = O / H
  • Preceding letter of C is "A" and succeding letter is "H", hence
    cos θ = A / H
  • Preceding letter of T is "O" and succeding letter is "A", hence
    tan θ = O / A

Trigonometic ratios

  • sin θ = opp / hyp
  • cos θ = adj / hyp
  • tan θ = opp / adj
  • cosec θ = hyp / opp
  • sec θ = hyp / adj
  • cot θ = adj / opp

INVERSE RATIOS

Inverse ratios show the relation of the
various trigonometic ratios.

  • sin θ = 1 / cosec θ
  • cos θ = 1 / sec θ
  • tan θ = 1 / cot θ
  • cosec θ = 1 / sin θ
  • sec θ = 1 / cos θ
  • cot θ = 1 / tan θ
